# Governance

SV-Gap is an independent open-source research project maintained by Shraddha S.
It is not an NVIDIA product, benchmark, or official company position. Employer
affiliations provide conflict-of-interest context and do not imply endorsement.
Contributors are responsible for obtaining any employer or institutional
approval required for their contributions.

SV-Gap is currently a maintainer-led early-stage project. Shraddha S is the
release maintainer and final arbiter for repository changes. Technical claims,
checker behavior, and research interpretations remain challengeable through
issues and pull requests.

## Decision records

Changes to any of the following require a documented pull-request rationale:

- manifest or report contracts;
- status semantics or gap denominators;
- built-in rule definitions;
- frozen taskpacks or claim-bearing artifacts;
- adjudication and blinding procedures; and
- release claim wording.

Frozen artifacts are never rewritten. Corrections create a new version and
retain a side-by-side explanation.

## Backend and evidence neutrality

The project does not require contributors to treat the reference backend as
ground truth. Competing open or external backends, false-result reports, and
adjudication records are first-class contributions. Maintainers may reject a
backend that hides inconclusive states, lacks redistribution clarity, or
overstates what its findings establish.

## Becoming a maintainer

Regular contributors who demonstrate careful claim discipline, review changes
outside their own contributions, and maintain a taskpack or backend may be
invited as maintainers. A future multi-maintainer project will document voting,
release, and conflict-of-interest procedures before transferring release
authority.

## Conflicts of interest

Authors and reviewers should disclose employer, model-provider, checker-vendor,
or benchmark-maintainer relationships relevant to a claim-bearing contribution.
Affiliation does not invalidate evidence; undisclosed dependencies make its
independence difficult to interpret.
